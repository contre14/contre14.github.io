Lunar Rover — Thermal Control System

Aug 2023 – May 2024

Project Manager & Thermal Systems Lead · 7-person team

California State University, Long Beach — Senior Design (Team Auger)

Our rover had to operate inside a permanently-shadowed lunar crater — no sunlight for warmth, surface temperatures from −205 °C to 100 °C, and avionics that had to stay inside a survivable band for the whole mission. I led the thermal design that kept them there, and managed the 7-person team through all four design reviews.
  • With no sun, the problem flips from collecting heat to managing it — I built the design around a radioisotope heater unit (RHU) as a steady source and sized everything to reject the excess.
  • Designed the radiator panels in SolidWorks, iterating the geometry to ~22 ft² so heat-flux density dropped ~59% — the margin needed to hold the survival band.
  • A flexible heat-pipe loop (FHPL) with a thermal switch moves waste heat from the electronics box to the radiators only when needed, so the box doesn't overcool in the cold case.
  • Wrote the thermal-balance / vacuum test plan, and used mass/power/cost trade studies to steer the design — which also cut estimated project cost ~35%.

Thermochemical Nonequilibrium Shock Modeling

Dec 2025

Independent Project · N₂/N Two-Temperature Formulation

University of California, Irvine

Behind a strong shock — a hypersonic forebody, or the high-enthalpy flow in a nozzle — gas heats so fast that its energy modes fall out of step: the molecules are translating hot before their vibrational energy catches up. A single-temperature model can't see that gap. I built a 1-D nitrogen model that tracks two temperatures separately so it can.
  • Rankine–Hugoniot jump conditions set the state across the shock; a coupled ODE solver then marches species, velocity, and the translational and vibrational temperatures downstream.
  • The two temperatures relax toward each other over distance until the flow re-equilibrates — the gap between them is the nonequilibrium I was chasing.
  • Post-processed into engineering plots and energy-mode (rotational / vibrational / electronic) population distributions to sanity-check the physics.
