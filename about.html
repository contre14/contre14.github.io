About me

I'm an aerospace engineer focused on propulsion and thermal-fluid systems. My work spans experimental combustion research, computational shock-layer modeling, and full-lifecycle hardware projects — and it's consistently about the same thing: making complex analysis repeatable, documented, and trustworthy.

I've automated MATLAB data-reduction pipelines for 100+ high-speed test videos, built parameter-sweep simulation workflows on Linux in Python, and led a lunar-rover senior design team as project manager and thermal systems lead. Along the way I co-authored a peer-reviewed paper in npj Advanced Manufacturing and was a two-time NSF LSAMP researcher.

I'm now looking for industry roles where I can apply propulsion, thermal, and systems engineering to real hardware — and keep raising the bar on how carefully the analysis behind it is done.
