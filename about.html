About me

I'm an aerospace engineer drawn to propulsion and thermal-fluid systems — the problems where the physics is unforgiving and the only way to know you're right is to build it and test it. What I care about most is rigor: analysis that's repeatable, documented, and honest about its assumptions, so the next person can trust it and build on it.

I led an 8-person lunar-rover senior design team as project manager and thermal systems lead, designing the radiator system in SolidWorks and writing the thermal-vacuum test plan. I've built a two-temperature shock-layer model in Python/MATLAB, automated MATLAB data-reduction pipelines for 100+ high-speed combustion test videos, and co-authored a peer-reviewed paper in npj Advanced Manufacturing.

I'm a U.S. citizen (ITAR-eligible) seeking Fall 2026 propulsion and thermal-fluids internships where I can apply this to real flight hardware — heat transfer, fluids, test rigs, and integration.
