Research

Experimental and computational research in combustion, soft matter, and high-temperature flows.

Jun 2021 – May 2024 · CSU Long Beach

NSF Researcher — Space & Combustion Lab

Undergraduate researcher under Dr. Joseph Kalman

I ran benchtop experiments on how non-Newtonian binder fluids wet and spread under oscillatory forcing — behavior relevant to solid-rocket propellant manufacturing. Using a droplet-impact test rig and a high-speed camera, I automated MATLAB data reduction for 100+ test videos, extracting time-resolved spreading metrics and standardized plots and cutting processing time ~50%. The work fed directly into a peer-reviewed publication.

Jun 2022 – Jul 2022 · UC Berkeley

Inclusive Excellence Researcher — Structural Color

Summer research fellow · self-assembling nanoparticles at fluid interfaces

I worked on manipulating the volume fraction of colloidal-particle droplets to create structural color — color produced by particle spacing rather than pigment. I built double-emulsion setups (DI-water / ETPTA / DI-water) to assemble the particles, processed microscopy data in MATLAB to quantify droplet size and volume fraction, and prototyped microfluidic device concepts in SolidWorks. I presented results to 50+ attendees at the Berkeley 2022 Summer Symposium.

Graduate Researcher — High-Temperature Flow Modeling

M.S. thesis research · propulsion-relevant nonequilibrium flows
