Jan 2026 – Present · UC Irvine

Graduate Researcher — High-Temperature Flow Modeling

M.S. thesis research · propulsion-relevant nonequilibrium flows

Studying nonequilibrium post-shock flows means running the same analysis across dozens of conditions and trusting that the differences between cases are real physics — not run-to-run noise. My research is building the workflow that makes that trust earned.

What I built

  • Scripted the whole loop in Python on Linux — case generation, batch execution, output parsing — so a full parameter sweep runs unattended (~60% less turnaround across 50+ cases)
  • Tracked down the run failures that quietly corrupt sweeps and pinned the post-processing to fixed metrics, so re-runs hold <2% variation and cross-case comparisons stay apples-to-apples
  • The two-temperature shock model above grew out of this line of work

Jun 2021 – May 2024 · CSU Long Beach

NSF Researcher — Space & Combustion Lab

Undergraduate researcher under Dr. Joseph Kalman

In Dr. Kalman's lab I studied how polybutadiene binders — the fluids used to 3D-print solid rocket propellant — wet and spread when a droplet strikes a surface. The catch: the event is over in milliseconds, so every measurement has to come from high-speed video, not a ruler.

What I did

  • Ran the benchtop droplet-impact experiments and high-speed imaging — following standardized procedures and keeping QC records so the data actually repeated run to run
  • Wrote the MATLAB pipeline that turned 100+ raw videos into quantitative spreading data (~50% less processing time) — the measurements our paper is built on
  • The full pipeline and the techniques behind it are on the publication →

Jun 2022 – Jul 2022 · UC Berkeley

Inclusive Excellence Researcher — Structural Color

Summer research fellow · self-assembling nanoparticles at fluid interfaces

Structural color is color made by geometry instead of pigment — pack tiny particles at just the right spacing and they reflect a specific wavelength. My summer project was learning to control that spacing by tuning the volume fraction of colloidal droplets.

What I did

  • Built double-emulsion setups (DI-water / ETPTA / DI-water) to self-assemble the particles into droplets
  • Measured droplet size and volume fraction from microscopy in MATLAB, and prototyped microfluidic devices in SolidWorks for steadier, leak-resistant flow
  • Presented the results to 50+ attendees at the Berkeley 2022 Summer Symposium
